⏰ The 90-Minute Nap Calculator

Based on the Basic Rest-Activity Cycle (BRAC), sleep cycles run ~90 minutes. Wake during deep sleep? Groggy for hours. Wake during REM? Fresh as a daisy.

Pro Tip: You'll wake up naturally at better times if you fall asleep at one of these windows. Avoid waking during the middle of a sleep cycle (that's where the grogginess comes from).

Sleep Cycle Breakdown

20-Minute Nap (One Stage)

Light NREM sleep. Good for alertness boost. Avoid if possible—too short, groggy wakeup.

90-Minute Nap (Full Cycle)

Light NREM → Deep NREM → REM. Includes full memory consolidation AND emotional processing. Best nap length.

180-Minute Nap (2 Cycles)

Two full cycles. For emergency recovery days when you've missed major sleep. Go for 90 min if possible (you'll feel better faster).

💤 Understanding Sleep Debt

Sleep debt is cumulative. If you need 8 hours/night and get 5, you're 3 hours in debt. After three nights, that's 9 hours of debt. Your brain doesn't forgive or forget—it compounds.

Recovery Timeline

1-3 hours debt: Recovered in 1 good night (8 hours sleep)
5-10 hours debt: Takes 2-3 good nights to recover
15+ hours debt: Takes 5-7+ good nights. This is why that "sleep when you're dead" mentality destroys your performance.
Chronic (50+ hours over months): Your baseline neurotransmitter levels shift. Recovery takes weeks. Seek professional help if depressed/anxious.

🛏️ The Ultimate Dorm-Room Sleep Hygiene Checklist

Sleep hygiene is all about entraining your biological clock to the 24-hour cycle. Here is how to bio-hack your dorm room.

🌡️ The Core Body Temp (CBT) Hack

To fall asleep quickly, your core body temperature actually needs to drop. Keep your dorm room cool at night. The hormone melatonin helps adjust your CBT set-point, pushing blood to your extremities so your core can cool down and you can "chill out".

💻 Kill the Blue Light

Texting in bed or staring at your laptop directly suppresses melatonin production, messing up your circadian rhythms.

🏃‍♂️ Watch Your Workout Time

Regular exercise is great for sleep, but you should completely avoid heavy exercise within 4 hours of your bedtime.

😴 Be Careful With Naps

Think a late afternoon nap will save you? Napping too late in the day can increase the time it takes you to fall asleep at night and actively decreases your essential slow-wave sleep.

🛏️ The "Bed is for Sleep" Rule

Do not study, watch TV, or eat in your bed. You need to behaviorally condition your brain to associate your mattress strictly with sleep and relaxation.
